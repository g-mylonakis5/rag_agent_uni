AML: Adversarial Machine Attack

Evasion Attacks,Poisoning Attacks,Privacy attacks 

Evasion: To manipulate input data in a way that causes  a ML model to make incorrect predictions.
Poisoning: To manipulate the training data used to train a model, compromising its performance or introducing vulnerabilities
Privacy: To extract sensitive info from models or training data ,compromising the confidentiality of the system

if an autonomous self-driving car that uses a traffic sign detection system is compromised by adversaries, it can result in the car falsely detecting a stop sign as a speed limit sign.

The attacks can take place before the testing, during the testing or after the release of the model.

Targeted attacks involve the calculation of an adversarial example that tricks the AI system into doing a specific action (installing spyware, forcing system to shut down)

Untargeted attacks try to cause random harm to the system (tricking the system into misclassifying visuals or noises)
