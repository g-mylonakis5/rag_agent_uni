Despite missing Sasha Vezenkov and Thomas Walkup, Olympiacos was firing at all cylinders on the way to an easy win
Olympiacos tied Valencia in second place in the EuroLeague standings after a night to remember.
The Greeks got their 20th win of the season on a premature contest for the 34th Round, dominating Paris on the road on the way to an impressive 104-87 win.
Despite missing Vezenkov and Walkup, Olympiacos was simply on fire all night.
Tyler Dorsey scored 24 points, Alec Peters had 18, and Evan Fournier added 17, as the Greeks finished the night with 17 made threes out of 30.
Justin Robinson had 18 points for Paris, which dropped to an 11-19 record after a game that was all about offense.
Olympiacos led by 13 points (33-20) seconds before the end of the first period, shooting 80% in three-pointers (8/10). Still, Partis made it even a four-point game (51-47), but Olympiacos closed the half leading (63-55) and with a new team record of 13 made threes in 19 attempts.
Olympiacos extended the lead to 11 points (71-60) and pushed it to 18 (87-69) in the closing second of the third quarter.
Nothing changed until the end, as Paris made it a 10-point game (87-77) and couldn’t get any closer, as Olympiacos got even to a 20-point advantage (104-84).
