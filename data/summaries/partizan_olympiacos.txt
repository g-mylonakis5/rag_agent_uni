Belgrade – Partizan continues to struggle to find rhythm since coach Željko Obradović stepped aside, facing an in-form Olympiacos side that recorded its fifth win in the last six EuroLeague games.
Led by Sasha Vezenkov, the Greek team dominated on the road, securing a commanding 104-66 victory at Aleksandar Nikolić Hall. Olympiacos improved to 13-8 in the standings, while Partizan remained at the bottom with a 6-16 record after their seventh consecutive defeat.
Vezenkov scored 25 points for Olympiacos, with Thomas Walkup, Tyler Dorsey, Alec Peters, Nikola Milutinov, and Donta Hall each adding 12 points.
Partizan struggled to keep pace, though Dwayne Washington set a personal EuroLeague career-high with 28 points. Bruno Fernando was the only other Partizan player in double figures, contributing 11 points.
The hosts were competitive for just 10–15 minutes before Olympiacos gradually pulled away, extending their lead almost every minute. By the third quarter, a 30-point advantage made it clear that Olympiacos would cruise to a blowout win in Belgrade.
