After defeating Fenerbahçe, Olympiacos followed it up with another win, this time against Baskonia
Despite a slow start and a 19–24 deficit after the first quarter, Olympiacos responded in time, took control of the game, and eventually cruised to a second consecutive victory.
Following their win over Fenerbahçe in Piraeus, Georgios Bartzokas’ side also protected home court against Baskonia, prevailing 90–80.
Sasha Vezenkov led the way with 20 points and six rebounds, while Nikola Milutinov added 17 points and six boards. Tyler Dorsey and Tyrique Jones contributed 15 points each.
On the other side, Eugene Omoruyi stood out with 18 points for Baskonia. Timothé Luwawu-Cabarrot added 14, while Mamadi Diakite and Trent Forrest chipped in 11 points apiece.
